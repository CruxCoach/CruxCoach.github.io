Looking for a Kilter Board app alternative?

CruxCoach is a free, open-source Android app for the Kilter Board — and for the MoonBoard, Tension Board and four more systems. It needs no account, no Google services, and it can import the logbook you thought you'd lost.

What happened to the old Kilter app

On 26 March 2026, Aurora's Kilter app was pulled from the App Store and Play Store after a dispute between Aurora Climbing and Kilter, LLC. Tens of thousands of climbers lost access to their playlists, logbooks and draft climbs overnight, and the official replacement shipped half-finished. The episode made one thing obvious: when your training history lives on someone else's server, it can disappear without notice.

What CruxCoach does differently

  • No account, no signup. Your identity is a Nostr keypair generated on your phone — nothing anyone can revoke.
  • Your logbook lives on your device, in an encrypted SQLCipher database. It can't be taken down, because nobody else has it.
  • Open source under the GPL-3.0. Anyone can audit, fork and build it. The code is on Codeberg.
  • Works offline. The full climb catalogue — 496,000+ climbs across seven boards — is downloaded to your device on first sync.
  • Not just Kilter. One app drives the Kilter Board, MoonBoard, Tension, Grasshopper, Decoy, So iLL and Touchstone boards over Bluetooth LE.
  • No Google required. Runs on de-Googled phones — GrapheneOS, /e/OS, LineageOS without GApps.

And the honest part: CruxCoach is Android-only (8.0 or later, with Bluetooth LE), it is a community project not affiliated with Kilter, LLC or Aurora Climbing, and the MoonBoard 2024 set currently carries community-contributed problems rather than the full catalogue. If you need an iOS app, this isn't it.

Get your old Kilter data back

Your history from the discontinued app is recoverable. Aurora is obliged to hand over your personal data on request, and CruxCoach imports their export directly:

  1. Request your data export from Aurora

    Ask Aurora, the operator of the old Kilter app, for a personal data export. They email you a JSON file containing your ascents, attempts, circuits and bid history.

  2. Install CruxCoach

    Install CruxCoach on any Android 8.0+ phone — from Zapstore, or as a signed APK directly from Codeberg. No account is created at any point.

  3. Import the export file

    In CruxCoach, open Settings → Migrate from Aurora and pick the JSON file. Your history lands in your local, encrypted logbook. Re-importing the same file is safe — nothing is duplicated.

Still have a working Kilter account? Then you can skip the export: sign in once inside CruxCoach and it imports your logbook directly. Either way, your data then lives encrypted on your device — and stays exportable as JSON.
